# Conversion Principles

## Trust Building Techniques
- **Logo Walls**: Use REAL SVG logos (Simple Icons or devicon), not plain text wordmarks. Logo walls belong UNDER the hero section, not stuffed inside it.
- **Logo-Only Rule**: Display the logos and nothing else. Do NOT print industry/category labels below each logo (e.g., "Stripe" + "Payments").
- **Authentic Social Proof**: Keep quotes to 3 lines max. Always attribute with Name + Role + Company. Never use generic names like "John Doe" or "Jane Doe". 
- **Honest Data**: Avoid fake-precise numbers (e.g., `4.1x`, `48k`, `5.8mm`) unless they come from real brand data or are explicitly marked as mock data. Faking engineering precision reads as slop.

## CTA (Call to Action) Strategies
- **Single Intent Rule (No Duplicate Intent)**: Pick ONE label for an action and use it everywhere. Having "Get in touch", "Contact us", and "Let's talk" all on the same page dilutes focus. 
- **Accessibility & Contrast**: A CTA must be readable. No white text on white backgrounds or ghost buttons blending into photos. Ensure WCAG AA 4.5:1 ratio.
- **Button Wrap Ban**: Primary CTAs must never wrap to two lines on desktop. Maximize brevity (1-3 words).

## Persuasion & Content Patterns
- **Cut Ruthlessly**: The user judges based on the first impression, not a full read. The default content shape per section is a short headline (≤ 8 words) + short sub-paragraph (≤ 25 words) + one visual/CTA.
- **No Data-Dump Sections**: Avoid massive 20-row feature tables or pricing matrices. Group them into chunks, use cards, or show top highlights with a "View full list" link.
- **Split-Header Ban**: The pattern of a large left headline and a small right explainer paragraph is banned by default. Sections should have ONE focused message stacked vertically.

## Lead Generation Patterns (Forms)
- **Label Placement**: Labels go ABOVE the input. Helper text is optional. Error text goes BELOW.
- **Placeholder Ban**: NEVER use the placeholder as the input label. 
- **Visual Contrast**: Form inputs, focus rings, and placeholder texts must pass contrast checks against the background. 

## Copywriting & Tone (Self-Audit)
- **Eliminate AI Clichés**: Remove words like "Elevate", "Seamless", "Unleash", "Next-Gen", "Game-changer", "Delve", "Elegant".
- **Grammar & Referents**: Write plain, specific, functional sentences. AI-generated "cute" copy is worse than boring functional copy.

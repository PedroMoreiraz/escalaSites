# Content Rules

All copy generated for client sites must follow these rules.
Content is the primary conversion driver. These rules are not optional.

---

## Authenticity

- Every word must reflect the specific client. No generic industry copy.
- Research from `clients/[slug]/research/` must influence: headlines, positioning, CTAs.
- Do not reuse copy from other clients or from playbook templates verbatim.
- Never fabricate client credentials, awards, or social proof.

## Headlines

- Headlines answer a specific question or state a specific outcome.
- Forbidden words in headlines: "Elevate", "Transform", "Empower", "Unlock", "Seamless", "Solutions", "Next-level", "World-class".
- Hero headline maximum: 8 words.
- Subheadlines maximum: 14 words.
- No em-dashes (—) anywhere in copy. Use colons or restructure.

## Conversion Copy

- Trust before persuasion. Establish credibility before making a request.
- CTAs must be specific: "Book a Free Consultation" not "Contact Us".
- Every CTA must state the benefit or outcome, not the action alone.
- No fake urgency. No countdown timers unless there is a real deadline.
- No dark patterns.

## Structure

- The first sentence of any section must earn the reader's attention.
- Paragraphs maximum 3 sentences in body copy.
- Use active voice. Passive voice is permitted only for technical constraints.
- Lists (bullet points) maximum 6 items. More than 6 = restructure into subheadings.

## Tone

- Tone is defined by the client's sector playbook and brand profile.
- Formal sectors (law, finance, medical): precise, authoritative, no colloquialisms.
- Approachable sectors (restaurant, retail, wellness): warm, specific, conversational.
- Never mix tones within the same project.

## Localization

- Use the client's geographic dialect and terminology.
- Brazilian Portuguese projects: use `você`, not `tu`, unless regional dialect specifies otherwise.
- Currency, date formats, and phone formats must match the client's locale.
